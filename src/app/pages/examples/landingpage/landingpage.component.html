ECS 188
Ethics in an Age of Technology

This course examines moral reasoning, ethical theories, and their application to contemporary issues in technology. We will explore fundamental questions about what it means to make ethical decisions in an era of rapid innovation.

Schedule & Assigned Readings

We will cover 10 chapters from the main textbook (Gordon Graham, Theories of Ethics, 1st Edition), one chapter per week, as indicated below.

Week Topic Assigned Reading
1 Ethics, Truth, and Reason Chapter 1
2 Contractualism Chapter 2
3 Egoism Chapter 3
4 Hedonism Chapter 4
5 Naturalism and Virtue Chapter 5
6 Existentialism Chapter 6
7 Kantianism Chapter 7
8 Utilitarianism Chapter 8
9 Ethics and Environment Chapter 9
10 Ethics, Religion, and the Meaning of Life Chapter 10

Grading Scheme

Below is the breakdown of how your final grade will be determined.

Assignment Weight Details
Pop-up Quizzes (Individual) 15% One quiz each week (Weeks 1–9), lowest score dropped. Quizzes cannot be retaken. If you miss one for a valid, documented reason, it won't count against your grade.
Weekly Presentations (Team of 5) 25% Weeks 2–9 (8 weeks), each team presents on relevant ethical issues & readings, connecting them to applied ethics domains (Bioethics, Business Ethics, AI Ethics, etc.). 20–25 min with slides.
Term Paper Presentations (Team of 2) 25% Presentation in Week 10 on your final term paper and reflections on seminar takeaways.
Term Paper (Team of 2) 35% ~2000-word paper analyzing one (or multiple) ethical theories applied to a contemporary technological issue.
